# Assigning Roles

<aside>
📌 These day-of roles are separate from pre-event roles (like RM/ARMs).

At small events, one person may have multiple roles.

</aside>

## Code Evangelist®

**Who has this role:** This role is assigned by CodeDay and requires special training. If you want to become a Code Evangelist, reach out to your CodeDay contact.

**Responsible for:** creating the right culture, keeping things fun, making sure students are getting the right help.

**Supervises:** mentors, volunteers leading activities.

## Event Manager

**Who has this role:** This role is assigned by the RM. (And is often, but not always, the RM themselves!)

**Responsible for:** keeping on schedule; backend logistics (e.g. meals)

**Supervises:** volunteers for setup, meals, and supply runs.

## Mentors

**Who has this role:** Professional programmers who volunteer, as well as any floaters who are well-versed in programming.

**Responsible for:** helping students learn to think like a programmer!

## Floaters (General Volunteers)

**Who has this role:** At any point if you don’t have an assigned task, you are a floater.

**Responsible for:** Patrolling the event —

- **Talk to students** to learn what they’re working on and what excites them about technology. Talk about your own experiences.
- **Be a mentor.**
- **Look for students who seem to be frustrated,** and be/connect with a mentor.
- **Keep an eye out** for rules violations.
- **Clean** garbage from teams’ work areas.
