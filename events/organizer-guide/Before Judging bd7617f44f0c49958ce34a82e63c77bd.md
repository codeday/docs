# Before Judging

Because participants often don’t sleep as much as we’d like, they do poorly at presentations. To help remove that factor, **we ask judges to walk around from 9am-10am and try the games individually.**

Judges should start arriving around 9am. You should have the Code Evangelist talk to the judges as they arrive to explain the procedures. The CodeDay supplies kit includes a bunch of pens and [judging sheets](http://assets.srnd.org/codeday/judges.pdf) which you can give to the judges.

If you have a lot of participants, you can break judges up into multiple groups and start them at different locations. At least one judge needs to see each group.

If you have a lot of volunteers, you can also assign one to follow each judge group and help keep them moving.

The judging sheets also include space for notes and subjective (smiley-face) ratings for each team, to help speed up deliberation later.

# Deliberation

Judges should be taken to a side room to discuss awards **during presentations.** Judges don't need to see the presentations, and this makes everything go *much* faster.

# Awards

Judges need to give out awards for:

![AwardSticker_Best in Show.png](Before%20Judging/AwardSticker_Best_in_Show.png)

1x Best in Show (think “1st place”)

![AwardSticker_Best in Class.png](Before%20Judging/AwardSticker_Best_in_Class.png)

2x Best In Class, usually broken down into

- Best in Class - Game (think “2nd, in the game category”)
- Best in Class - App (think “2nd, in the app category”)
- You can technically give these out however you want. e.g. if there are a bunch of games, you could give out "Best in Class - Multiplayer Game" and "Best in Class - Singleplayer Game"

![AwardSticker_Special Prize.png](Before%20Judging/AwardSticker_Special_Prize.png)

2x (ish) Special Prizes, for anything they want to recognize. Common examples of special prizes are:

- **0 to 60**: for a team with no/little experience who tried their best and made something amazing!
- **Spirit of CodeDay**: for a team who used the theme really well, participated in a lot of workshops/activities, or otherwise showed large amounts of creativity at the event

<aside>
📌 Judges do NOT give out the “World Champion” awards — CodeDay HQ will give those out in an online ceremony.

</aside>
