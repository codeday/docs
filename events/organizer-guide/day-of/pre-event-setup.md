# Pre-Event Setup

Your Event Manager should communicate a specific time for this; 10:30am at the latest.

Setup is not just about logistics, it's also about making sure the venue is prepared for people to be social.

{: .important }
Despite what the website says, CodeDay does **not** start at noon -- it starts as soon as the first attendee is in the door. Once attendees are in the door, there *must* be someone focused on providing a positive experience and building the right event culture.

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] **Load-in:** supplies, snacks, and drinks.
- [ ] **Establish a storage area.** Mark the area as staff-only. Remove all the chairs from this room so volunteers don't hide from the attendees.
- [ ] **Arrange tables and chairs.** Try to create large rooms where 50-120 attendees will work.
- [ ] **Label off-limits areas.** Mark conference rooms as off-limits unless the seats are absolutely needed (we want them in the main area.) Block off access to any parts of the venue students aren't allowed in.
- [ ] **Find climate controls. (If any.)** Many venues don't have their climate controls set properly for the evening. Ask if there's a way you can change the temperature if it gets too hot/cold.
- [ ] **Set-up the checkin area.** Ensure people can't walk in and out without passing directly by you for security and safety reasons. Set up swag, shirts, etc. Set up a laptop (or multiple) with Checkin loaded from Clear.
- [ ] **Hang signs.** This includes wayfinding (arrows and direction signs), after-hours call signs, and the Code of Conduct signs (near check-in).
- [ ] **Connect to the projector.** Ensure both video and sound work. Load the intro display from the day-of section of Clear.

## Photos and Videos

If you have a photographer or a videographer, here's a suggested list of shots to get:

- Event Collateral B-Roll
    - Event signs
    - Swag
    - Anything with sponsor logos
- Crowd B-Roll
    - Venue establishing shots, showing scale of event
    - Students working in groups
    - Students in workshops
    - Students doing fun activities
    - Shot from stage at kickoff and closing
- Projects B-Roll
    - Presentations
    - Student computers close-up when working
    - Student typing on keyboards close-up
- Speakers
    - Organizers at kickoff
    - Workshop presenters
    - End presentations
    - Any sponsors speaking
- Award Presentations
- Soundbites
    - Students
        - "describe your project for us"
        - "did you have a plan going into codeday?"
        - "how did you get people together for a team?"
        - "what inspired you to come to CodeDay?"
        - "what have you learned during codeday?"
        - "what do you like about coding?"
        - "favorite thing about codeday?"
        - "how was your first codeday experience?"
        - (artists/musicians) "how do art and code go hand in hand?"
    - Volunteers
        - "Why do you volunteer?"
        - "How does helping students make you feel?"
        - "What do you do as a volunteer?"
        - "What's your favorite thing about CodeDay?"
