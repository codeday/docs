# Internship/Job Info Session

Thanks for your interest in hosting an info-session for an internship or job opening! This document will walk you through how to prepare and what to expect!

## Format

- **Location:** Zoom Webinar
- **Duration:** 15-30min Presentation + 15min Q&A
- **Schedule:**
    - Any day of the week is fine
    - For local opportunities, after 1pm is best
    - For nationwide opportunities, between 2pm-4pm Pacific is best

CodeDay will set up and host the webinar. Please plan to arrive 10 minutes early for a tech check.

You will be able to screen-share.

With your permission, we will simulcast your info session to our Twitch channel (twitch.tv/codeday_org) which is easier for some students to access.

We will introduce you at the start of the program, and then turn it over to you. Before we start the webinar, we'll also ask you to decide if you'd like our host to ask you Q&A questions, or if you want to handle them entirely on your own.

## CodeDay Demographics

CodeDay community members are split between high school and college students. About half of our college students are looking for full-time employment, and half are looking for internships.

**High School Students**

- 60% are a member of a group underrepresented in technology
- 40% are women.
- 32% are Black, Latine, or Native American.
- 36% are from a low-income background.

**College Students**

- 70% are a member of a group underrepresented in technology.
- 65% are women.
- 33% are Black, Latine, or Native American.
- 35% are from a low-income background.
- 65% are from non-target recruiting schools (community colleges, trade schools, and other local, non-PhD granting school).

**We understand not all companies are hiring all of our students.** In particular high school students. Let us know who you're looking for (anyone, college interns, and/or entry level full-time) and we will promote to the right people.

## Suggested Format

You're welcome to use whatever format you'd like for a presentation, but here's one that we've seen work well in the past:

- About your company — make sure to explain what do you do at a high-level
- Job that you are hiring for:
    - A description of the specific work
    - Qualifications
    - If this is an internship, what is the duration start/end?
- What do you look for when reviewing an application? (What sorts of experience should students make sure to emphasize?)
- What are some past examples of interesting projects engineers in this role have worked on? (If this is a new role, what are some interesting problems engineers in your company solve?)
- What support structure is in place to help newer engineers grow in their career? (i.e. how does your company mentor newer engineers?)
- Describe your team and/or company culture. (i.e. why is this a great place to work?)
- Specific next steps to apply

## Next Steps

Please provide:

1. A few times you are available. (Keep in mind the ideal times listed under Format above, if possible.)
2. A list of the names an emails of who will be talking at the info session, so that we can invite them.
3. 1-paragraph short description of your company + the position(s) you're hiring for.
4. Qualifications for the position(s) you're hiring for.
5. Link to job posting, if you have one. Otherwise, a link to your careers page or homepage.

We will send you a calendar invite, and start promoting this to the relevant students.
