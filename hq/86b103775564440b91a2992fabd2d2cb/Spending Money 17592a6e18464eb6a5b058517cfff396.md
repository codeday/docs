## General Expense Requirements

### Submit Timely Expense Reports

Expense reports and reimbursement requests need to be submitted within 60 days of when you incurred the expense, unless there's a specific reason you needed to wait. ("I forgot" is not a good reason.) The IRS requires us to deny expenses that are not submitted in a timely fashion.

### Keep Your (Itemized!) Receipts

The IRS requires us to have *itemized* receipts for all expenses, whether they're reimbursed or charged to our corporate card. "Itemized" means it shows what was actually bought, not just the total amount.

**In Abacus (Reimbursements)**
You'll need to upload your receipt to submit your expense.

**In Divvy (Corporate Card)**
Once you make a charge, Divvy will send you a push notification asking you to upload a receipt. It will also show up as "incomplete" until the receipt is uploaded.

**If you lost a receipt for an expense under $75,** you can create and submit your own itemized receipt. Make sure to include the vendor, date, and an itemized list of purchases and costs. Include a screenshot from your credit card statement if possible. 

**If you lost a receipt for a larger expense,** please contact the vendor and try to get a copy of the receipt. If you cannot get a receipt, you can submit the following affidavit instead. Please include screenshots from your credit card statement. Excessive use may result in denial of future expenses.

[MissingReceiptForm.pdf](Spending%20Money/MissingReceiptForm.pdf)

[MissingReceipt.odt](Spending%20Money/MissingReceipt.odt)

### "Embedded" Expenses

Embedded expenses are those in which a personal expense is incurred in the same transaction as a business expense.

**In Abacus (Reimbursements)**
Please try to avoid embedded expenses as much as possible.

When an embedded expense is incurred, you must be cautious to remove it from the total amount, *including associated taxes.* Please include information about your calculations in the "notes" field.

**In Divvy (Corporate Card)**
Embedded expenses are not acceptable on corporate cards. Follow the mistaken expense policy to reimburse CodeDay for any accidental embedded expenses, *including associated taxes.*

### Mistaken Expenses

If you accidentally submit a personal expense, or incur a personal expense on an CodeDay Corporate Card, you must reimburse CodeDay for the difference within 3 business days of when you notice it, or are notified that a personal expense was incurred.

Please submit payments as follows:

- Make a payment for the expense amount (including associated taxes) at https://www.codeday.org/donate. Do not select any gifts.
- Forward your receipt to accounts@codeday.org.
- As your payment did not constitute a donation, we will void your donation receipt.

## Day-to-Day Expenses

### Ongoing Business Expenses

CodeDay employees often incur expenses in the course of business, such as office rent, and information technology. Large expenses, or recurring expenses should be pre-approved by someone in that employee's management hierarchy.

CodeDay employees may never sign a contract or agreement for these services unless they are an authorized approver.

### Event Expenses

CodeDay will pay for event expenses only as included in an event budget submitted and approved to a CodeDay employee in the submitter's management hierarchy. Common event expenses include:

- Participant meals.
- Event supplies, such as name tags or board games.
- Venue fees.
- Volunteer travel reimbursements.

Emergency expenses — up to the lesser of $100 or 10% of the event budget — may be incurred without prior approval. A common example would be a missing A/V adapter for a presenter, or a meal for a student with special dietary restrictions.

### Meeting Over Coffee or Food

Except when incurred as a per diem expense, the IRS requires that food and drink expenses be substantiated by the "Five Ws":

1. **Who** was there?
2. **Where** did the meal occur?
3. **When** did the meal occur?
4. **What** was the cost of the meal?
5. **Why** was the meal considered official business?

Typically, you a receipt will meet requirements 2-4, however you must provide information about *who was present,* and *why the meal was official business.* For example:

> *Volunteer meetup for CodeDay Atlanta Winter 2019 volunteers.*
> 

**Documenting Meeting Food and Drink Expenses (Abacus/Divvy)**
Provide the required information about attendees and the purpose of the meal in the "Note" field on Abacus/Divvy.

Meals may commonly be provided:

- As part of a meetup or volunteer appreciation dinner.
- As part of a planning meeting.
- For program attendees.

You cannot expense your individual food purchases consumed in the act of working, unless as part of a per diem or if they're part of a meal provided to a business-related group (such as a CodeDay meal).

### Stolen or Damaged Items

CodeDay will pay for stolen or damaged venue property. CodeDay will also pay for replacement property owned by a participant, volunteer, or employee and stolen or damaged at an CodeDay-hosted event or activity:

- **Participants:** Stolen property up to a maximum of $100.
- **Volunteers and Employees:** Stolen or damaged property up to a maximum of $500.

**Documenting Stolen or Damaged Reimbursements (Abacus/Divvy)**
Information on the reason for reimbursement must be documented first to safety@codeday.org. A reference to this report should be included in the "Note" field on Divvy or Abacus.

For cash reimbursements, the original receipt or a listing from a reputable vendor showing current value can substantiate the expense. (An original receipt may be required at the discretion of accounting.)

CodeDay will provide cash reimbursements at the lower of the repair cost, current replacement cost, or the original purchase price.

CodeDay will only provide an in-kind replacement where the item cannot be repaired, and the cost of the item has appreciated by no more than 10% of the original purchase price. CodeDay reserves the right to replace an item with a newer version if the cost is lower.

### Books

For employees, CodeDay will pay for up to $250/yr of books (or audiobooks) with any relation to furthering their career or job performance. (That said, consider checking your public library first!)

### Participant Support Costs

CodeDay will pay for expenses which promote its primary mission, while conferring an ancillary benefit on one of its members. For example, promoting an app created at CodeDay may be done for the primary benefit of attracting students to CodeDay, even if the student may receive additional downloads of their app or personal promotion.

CodeDay cannot (legally) pay for any expenses where the primary benefit is to an individual.

**Documenting Benefit Expenses (Abacus/divvy)**
An explanation of how the expense is primarily business-related must be present in the "Note" field of Abacus/Divvy in order for your expense to be approved.

Because providing private benefits can jeopardize CodeDay's tax-exempt status, approval of expenses of this type is not guaranteed. If you are unsure if an expense will qualify, you should obtain pre-authorization by emailing accounts@codeday.org with an explanation of your expense.
