# Notes

- With ISS Approval: student visaholders must obtain pre-approval from their institution’s International Student Services office
- With USCIS Approval: spouses of some visaholders may work with pre-approval. More information at: [https://www.uscis.gov/employment-authorization](https://www.uscis.gov/employment-authorization)
- 9/5/6 Rule: foreign nationals in B-1, B-2, VWB, and VWT status may accept an honorarium and/or reimbursement of travel expenses for "usual academic activity or activities" if spending 9 days or less at CodeDay and if the individual has accepted such payment from no more than 5 educational or research institutions (including CodeDay) in the previous 6-month period

# Withholding Requirements

U.S. source nonemployee compensation must be withheld at a rate of 0%, 14%, or 30%:

- Required tuition and fees, including required books and course materials, are exempt from withholding as a “qualified portion” of the scholarship as long as the recipient is a nonresident alien student, researcher, or scholar on an F-1, J-1, M-1, or Q-1 visa type. Cash awards are not considered “qualified”.
- The recipient is entitled to a reduced withholding rate of 14% on the taxable (”non-qualified”) portion of the scholarship as long as they are a nonresident alien student, researcher, or scholar on an F-1, J-1, M-1, or Q-1 visa type. Cash awards for academic performance are treated as eligible for reduced withholding since cash awards are “awarded” for a future or continuing educational activity, thereby classifying them scholarships.
- Nonresidents who are not here on visa types F-1, J-1, M-1, or Q-1 are not entitled to exempt withholding, per § 1441 of the Internal Revenue Code and are subject to the full 30% tax liability.
