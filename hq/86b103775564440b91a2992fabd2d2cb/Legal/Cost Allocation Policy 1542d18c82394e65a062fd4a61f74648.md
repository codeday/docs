# Cost Allocation Policy

# General Accounting Policies

- **Basis of Accounting:** Accrual
- **Fiscal Period:** July 1 through June 30
- **Allocation Basis for Individual Cost Elements: Direct Allocation Basis**
- **Indirect Cost Rate Allocation Base:** MTDC
- **Internal Controls:** Adequate internal controls are maintained to ensure no costs are charged both directly and indirectly. Costs and revenues are accumulated in accounts coded for the purpose and type of expense. Costs are required to be supported with appropriate documentation.

# Cost Allocation Methodology

## Salaries and Wages

Documented with timesheets showing time distribution for all employees and allocated based on time spent on each program or grant

- **Direct Work:** Where work is specifically identifiable to specific grant / contract / program, employee salaries and wages are charged directly to the program for which work has been done.
- **Indirect Work:** Some employees perform necessary services indirectly benefiting the activities of the organization as a whole, and therefore charge their time as indirect costs.
- **Mixed Work:** Some employees divide their time across specific grants and activities which benefit the organization as a whole. These employees charge their salary costs to direct costs where work is specifically identifiable to specific grants / programs / funding, and indirect costs when it benefits the organization as a whole.

## Benefits

Benefits — including without limitation FICA, UC, Worker’s Compensation, Health Insurance, Dental Insurance, Life Insurance, Disability Insurance, Holiday, Vacation, and Sick Pay — are allocated in the same manner as salaries and wages.

## Professional Services Costs

Professional service costs are allocated in the same manner as salary and wages. 

Accounting and audit fees are recorded as indirect costs, while legal fees may benefit all programs and be charged indirectly, or may be provided for one specific program and be charged directly.

## Travel Costs

Travel costs may be charged as either direct or indirect costs depending on the purpose of the trip:

- **Direct Travel:** Travel costs are charged directly to the grant / contract / program for which the travel was incurred when possible. Travel costs for conferences to secure volunteers that benefit multiple grants / contracts / programs will be allocated based on the ratio of volunteers signed up for each program. Other travel costs that benefit more than one grant / contract / program will be allocated based on the salary allocation of the individual traveling.
- **Indirect Travel:** Travel costs that benefit the organization as a whole are charged as indirect costs.

## Board Expenses

Costs related to board meetings are charged as indirect costs and are limited to expenses allowed under federal travel regulations.

## Office Expense and Supplies

To the maximum extent possible, office costs such as postage, material and supplies are direct charged to the grant / contract / program using the item. Postage, materials and supplies used by staff engaged in indirect activities are charged on an indirect basis.

## Occupancy Expenses

General maintenance and repair costs are recorded as indirect costs.

Building rental costs are allocated based on square footage and are charged as direct and indirect costs based on whether the space is occupied by staff whose salaries are directly charged or by staff whose salaries are indirectly charged. Costs for space occupied by staff whose salaries are charged on a mixed basis are allocated on a mixed basis in the same ratio as their salaries are allocated.

For all common areas, such as conference rooms, reception areas, hallways and restrooms, costs are recorded as indirect costs.

## Utilities

Natural gas and electric utilities are allocated at the same ratio as occupancy expenses.

## Communications

To the maximum extent possible, communication expenses such as email, text messages, or postcards are direct charged to the grant / contract / program sending the communications. General staff internet, telephone, and fax lines are charged as indirect expenses.

## Printing

Printing is allocated in the same manner as Communications.

## Insurance

Where an insurance risk is unique to a specific grant / contract / program, the cost of the insurance will be direct charged. Insurance that benefits the organization as a whole will be charged as an indirect expense.

## Capital Items

CodeDay depreciates equipment when the initial acquisition cost exceeds $5,000. Items below $5,000 are reflected in the supplies category and expensed in the current year.

The purchase price of capital items is charged directly to programs only if a contract or grant specifically authorizes such charges.  The cost of capital items purchased with non-Federal funds, but used in a manner benefitting grants / contracts / programs is recovered indirectly through depreciation charges.

Depreciation costs for allowable equipment used solely by one program are charged directly to the program using the equipment. If more than one grant / contract / program uses the equipment, then an allocation of the depreciation costs will be based on the expected ratio of use of the equipment.

## Unallowable Costs

Internal controls have been established to ensure costs that are unallowable in accordance with OMB Circular A-122 — including alcoholic beverages, bad debts, advertising (other than help-wanted ads), contributions, entertainment, fines and penalties — are not charged to federal funding sources.
