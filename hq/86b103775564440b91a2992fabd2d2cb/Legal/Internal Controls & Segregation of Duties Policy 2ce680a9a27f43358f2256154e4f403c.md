# Internal Controls & Segregation of Duties Policy

# General Policy

The Board of Directors is responsible for authorizing all bank accounts and check signers.
Financial institutions where CodeDay accounts are maintained are notified on an annual basis
of any changes in check signers, following the transition of officers or changes in staff with
check signing responsibilities. Financial reports shall be presented to the Board of Directors for review on a monthly basis. A financial compilation, review, or audit, depending upon the size of the budget, will be conducted annually. Applicable financial and administrative guidelines relating to specific grant funding shall be followed.

# Transaction Types

## Deposits

CodeDay has established systems that ensure all deposits are properly accounted for and cannot be diverted. Deposits should be made using these systems when possible:

- Electronic payments (including credit cards, ACH, and Wires) should be made through a Stripe Invoice.
- Checks should be mailed to CodeDay’s lockbox service at 440 N Barranca Ave, PMB 7763, Covina, CA 91723.

Incoming checks received at any other location must be restrictively endorsed, “for deposit only” with the organization’s account number, when received.

In no circumstances will cash deposits be accepted.

## Disbursements

CodeDay has established electronic systems that ensure all transactions are tied to a pre-approved budget and include receipts. Payments should be made using these systems when possible:

- Purchases should be made using a Divvy card associated with the relevant budget, with receipts submitted to Divvy within 7 days.
- Reimbursement should be submitted to on Abacus. Budget information and receipt must be included prior to purchase.

When necessary, check, wire, or ACH payments may be made with the authorization of anyone at VP level or higher. Payments will be submitted with associated budget information, invoice/backup, and approval information through Mercury Bill Pay.

In no cases will cash disbursements be made.

# Supporting Documentation

Documentation should be provided to provide clear evidence or support for each transaction.

- All expenses should include an itemized invoice, purchase order, and/or receipt showing the payee name and remittance information, the amount of the expense, and the purpose of the expense.
- All revenues and donations should include a sales receipt or transaction report showing the date of the transaction, the method and amount of payment, and the purpose of the payment, including any donor restrictions.
- Supporting documentation should be saved to an electronic location (such as Stripe, lockbox, Divvy, Abacus, or NetSuite) and retained indefinitely (no less than 7 years).

# Reconciliations

The organization’s financial accounts should be reconciled regularly to ensure that all transactions are properly approved, recorded, coded, and documented. Any errors or discrepancies should be researched and addressed in a timely manner.

- All bank accounts, credit card accounts, and other financial accounts should be reconciled every month.
- The financial institution’s monthly statements should be compared to accounting records and all available supporting documentation, checking for mathematical accuracy and proper categorization of each transaction.
- Any accounting errors, discrepancies, or omissions should be immediately researched, resolved, and documented.

# Authorizations and Approvals

Proper authorization and approval must be obtained before a transaction is processed.

Transactions may be authorized only by electronic approval. The approver’s password should never be shared with another person. Records of electronic approvals will be kept indefinitely (no less than 7 years). The approver is responsible for reviewing all supporting documentation and ensuring that the transaction is appropriate, accurate, and compliant with all of the organization’s policies, rules, and regulations.

No single person should have control over all parts of a transaction. Segregating these duties helps to reduce the organization’s risk of fraud and prevent errors or mistakes.

- The person who requests an expense should not approve that expense.
- The person who approves an expense should not maintain the accounting records.

# Segregation of Duties for Grant Funds

Day-to-day administration of grant funds, including all approvals for and review of expenditures, shall be overseen by an employee who (a) is not involved in the implementation of the grant; (b) does not draw their salary from grant funds, and; (c) to the extent possible, is not in the reporting hirearchy of anyone who receives grant funds.

Grants financials shall be reviewed regularly (no less than quarterly) by the cognizant committee of independent members of the Board of Directors.
