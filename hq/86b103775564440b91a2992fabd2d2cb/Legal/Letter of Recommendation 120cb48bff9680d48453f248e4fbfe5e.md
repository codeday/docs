# Letter of Recommendation

If you are a volunteer, mentor, employee, contractor, or Labs alum you can request a letter of recommendation from Tyler Menezes (CodeDay CEO) using the procedure below. You are also welcome to reach out to anyone else you’ve worked with at CodeDay, of course!

(Tyler cannot write LORs for in-person event participants unless he was personally at the event. Please ask the leader of the event for a LOR. If you don’t have their contact information, email [team@codeday.org](mailto:team@codeday.org) with the date and location of the event.)

Please email the information requested below with the subject line `LOR Request, due [date]` to [tylermenezes@codeday.org](mailto:tylermenezes@codeday.org) **AT LEAST TWO WEEKS PRIOR TO ANY DEADLINE** and before you submit a request into any portal (e.g., CommonApp, NIH, NSF, etc).

### Mentors/Volunteers/Employees

**Requirements:** You should be in good standing. Please cc your supervisor or event lead for verification (not required for mentors).

### CodeDay Labs Participants

**Requirements:** You should have met most program requirements (including stand-ups) and have generally positive feedback from your mentor about your time management and ability to work independently.

**Request Template:**

```
- LOR for: [role, company/school]
- Applying because: [why you think you are a good fit for this company/school & role]
- CodeDay history: [roles, dates]
- Two achievements I am most proud of at CodeDay:
    - [specific story/outcome]
    - [specific story/outcome]
- Anything else: [e.g., participation in other CodeDay events, journal clubs, volunteer leadership, etc]
```

**Request Template:**

```
- LOR for: [role, company/school]
- Applying because: [why you think you are a good fit for this company/school & role]
- Project(s): [desciption & dates]
- Two achievements I am most proud of on my projects:
    - [specific story/outcome]
    - [specific story/outcome]
- Anything else: [e.g., participation in other CodeDay events, journal clubs, volunteer leadership, etc]
```

<aside>
👉

For achievements, please list ***specific*** actions ***you*** took! e.g.:

GOOD: “Created promotion strategy and oversaw 10 volunteers promoting at schools”
BAD: “Event attended by 100 students”

</aside>
