# Payment Registration for Individual Grants

Some participants in CodeDay programs are eligible to receive payment(s) to help them dedicate more time to their education. (You will be specifically informed if this applies to you.)

These payments are handled by CodeDay Software Services LLC. You can expect to see this name rather than “CodeDay” on documents.

## Eligibility

CodeDay can currently only make grant payments to US residents with a tax ID. This includes citizens, greencard holders, and undocumented immigrants with a TIN. (See also the note below about tax reporting.)

Because we are not set up to handle withholding for grant payments, we cannot make payments to non-resident aliens such as F-1/J-1 student visa holders.

## Registering to Receive Payments

<aside>
📌 You will not be able to complete any of the following steps unless your account has been activated to receive payments. Please contact us at [accounts@codeday.org](mailto:accounts@codeday.org) if you are expecting to have access but do not.

</aside>

When logging in to your CodeDay account, you’ll be prompted to register for payouts. If you’re already logged in, just head over to [https://account.codeday.org/](https://account.codeday.org/) and scroll to the bottom.

![Screenshot 2024-04-26 at 11.32.27.png](Payment%20Registration%20for%20Individual%20Grants/Screenshot_2024-04-26_at_11.32.27.png)

Read the statements and check each to confirm that you understand. Once they’re all checked, the “Register for Payouts” button will become available.

![Screenshot 2024-04-26 at 11.32.36.png](Payment%20Registration%20for%20Individual%20Grants/Screenshot_2024-04-26_at_11.32.36.png)

Click the “Register for Payouts” button and you’ll be redirected to Stripe Connect:

![Screenshot 2024-04-26 at 11.32.57.png](Payment%20Registration%20for%20Individual%20Grants/Screenshot_2024-04-26_at_11.32.57.png)

Stripe will verify your identity and collect information to pay you and file tax forms. You must complete all of the following:

- Collecting address, Social Security Number (SSN), and date of birth
- Upload a photo of your ID
- Link your bank account

<aside>
📌 Stripe may ask you for a link to a website or professional social platform. If so, you can use a personal website if you have one, or your LinkedIn profile.

</aside>

It may take up to a week for Stripe to approve you for payments, and they may reach out to request additional information. Please email [accounts@codeday.org](mailto:accounts@codeday.org) if you have any questions about the process or the information Stripe has requested from you.

## Receiving Payments

Payments will be transferred into your Stripe account. The timing and amounts vary by program, so refer to the documents you were provided when joining the program for more details.

Once your payment has been transferred into your Stripe account, it usually takes 1-3 business days before it is transferred into your bank account.

To view payout details or update your bank information, access your Stripe account by visiting [https://account.codeday.org](https://account.codeday.org) and clicking on the Payouts Dashboard link near the bottom:

![Screenshot 2024-04-26 at 12.15.58.png](Payment%20Registration%20for%20Individual%20Grants/Screenshot_2024-04-26_at_12.15.58.png)

If you are no longer able to access the account you used to register for payments, please contact us at [accounts@codeday.org](mailto:accounts@codeday.org).

## Tax Liability

<aside>
⚠️ Please keep in mind you are most likely required to pay some amount of taxes on the money you receive. We cannot withhold these taxes for you!

***It’s your responsibility to set enough money aside for tax season.***

</aside>

If you received more than the filing threshhold (usually $400) in any given year, you can expect to receive either a 1099-MISC or 1099-NEC from CodeDay Software Services LLC in the mail in January or February. You can also access these documents electronically in your Stripe dashboard.

In addition to income tax, you may be liable for self-employment tax. While we cannot provide individual tax advice, please note our policy about how we report payments:

### **Grants to Degree-Seeking Students**

Grant payments are grants to perform research and studies primarily to further one’s own education, training, and academic excellence, and are not compensation subject to self-employment tax. Spiegelman v. Comm’r, 102 T.C. 394 (1994).  Accordingly, we report stipend payments for degree-seeking students on form 1099-MISC, Box 3 (Other income); the amount should be reported as “Other Income” on the individual’s tax return. Ruggiero v. Comm’r., 74 TCM 662 (1997). For more information on non-qualified scholarships and fellowships, see IRS Publication 970, “Tax Benefits for Education”

### **Non-Stipends and Stipends to Non-Students**

Stipend payments for non-students and non-degree seeking students may be subject to income tax and will be reported on form 1099-NEC. I.R.C. §117(a).
