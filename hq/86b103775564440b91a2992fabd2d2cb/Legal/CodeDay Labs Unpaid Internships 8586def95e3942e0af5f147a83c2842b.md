# CodeDay Labs Unpaid Internships

CodeDay Labs is solely an educational program. We use the term “internship” to make the experience understandable to employers, because the educational benefits most closely mirror those derived from a traditional internship. However, CodeDay Labs is an unpaid internship within DOL rules.

<aside>
🌐 **For International Students:**

Unpaid internships under DOL rules are not "employment" and therefore do not require a foreign student to be "employment authorized," i.e., F-1 OPT/CPT or J-1 Academic Training.

</aside>

Courts have used the “primary beneficiary test” to determine whether an intern or student is, in fact, an employee under the FLSA. This test allows courts to examine the “economic reality” of the relationship to determine which party is the “primary beneficiary” of the relationship.

Courts have described the “primary beneficiary test” as a flexible test, and no single factor is determinative. Accordingly, whether an intern or student is an employee under the FLSA necessarily 
depends on the unique circumstances of each case.

Courts have identified the following seven factors as part of the test:

### “The extent to which the intern and the employer clearly understand that there is no expectation of compensation.”

All participants in CodeDay Labs are provided many explanations that CodeDay Labs is not a paid internship. Most students do not receive any money for participation in CodeDay Labs. If money is provided, it is as an educational fellowship stipend funded by the school or government (1099-MISC), rather than compensation for work performed (W-2 or 1099-NEC).

### “The extent to which the internship provides training that would be similar to that which would be given in an educational environment.”

CodeDay Labs was designed to provide training similar to that which is given in an educational environment, as described in a number of academic papers such as “Open-Source Internships with Industry Mentors” (ITiCSE 2021).

### “The extent to which the internship is tied to the intern’s formal education program by integrated coursework or the receipt of academic credit.”

CodeDay Labs is generally integrated into formal education in several ways:

- Students attending partner institutions participate in integrated coursework, and receive academic credit for their participation.
- Students not attending partner institutions may still receive academic credit by requesting CodeDay Labs to complete their school’s appropriate documentation.

### “The extent to which the internship accommodates the intern’s academic commitments by corresponding to the academic calendar.”

CodeDay Labs is designed around academic commitments. During the school year, CodeDay provides 1-month, 10/hr/week experiences to fit with student classes, and only offers longer experiences over the summer when students do not have a significant class workload.

### “The extent to which the internship’s duration is limited to the period in which the internship provides the intern with beneficial learning.”

CodeDay Labs is designed to provide beneficial learning throughout the program. Students spend between 4-12 weeks working on a project of appropriate scope for the duration of their participation.

### “The extent to which the intern’s work complements, rather than displaces, the work of paid employees while providing significant educational benefits to the intern.”

The work of CodeDay Labs interns does not displace the work of paid employees. CodeDay does not maintain any employees whose responsibilities are contributing to Open Source Software, and does not derive any benefit (except incidentally) for the contributions students make to open-source software.

### “The extent to which the intern and the employer understand that the internship is conducted without entitlement to a paid job at the conclusion of the internship.”

CodeDay does not provide any paid jobs contributing to open-source software. The program is solely an educational experience.

### “[DOL] recognizes an exception for individuals who volunteer their time, freely and without anticipation of compensation, for religious, charitable, civic, or humanitarian purposes to non-profit organizations.”

CodeDay is a 501(c)(3) non-profit organization (Student Research and Development dba CodeDay, EIN 26-4742589), and contributions to Free, Open-Source Software are inherently charitable, civic, and humanitarian.
