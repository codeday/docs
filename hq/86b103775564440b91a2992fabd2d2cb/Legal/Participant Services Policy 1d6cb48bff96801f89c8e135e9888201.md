# Participant Services Policy

Unless otherwise specified in an approved grant proposal or contract, the following procedures apply when enrolling, tracking, and reporting data for participant services.

Participant services data will be stored in a CodeDay information system which is capable of storing rosters of participants and reportable individuals alongside training records for the required reporting period.

## Reportable Individual

Individuals become reportable if they take action that demonstrates an intent to use program services, including:

- Individuals who provide identifying information;
- Individuals who use only a self-service system; or
- Individuals who receive information-only services or activities.

## Performance Participants

Participants will be included in performance measures if they:

- Are determined to be eligible to participate in the program
- Receive a significant service (not entirely self-service or information-only) funded by the program at either a physical location or remotely through electronic technologies; and
- Exit the program.

## Participation Dates

Program participants will be tracked from the first date they receive a program-funded qualifying service. (”Date of Participation”) As such, the date of participation may be different across programs. Individuals participating in multiple programs will be counted as a participant in each of the programs.

Participants are deemed to have exited the program:

- When the participant is no longer eligible to receive a qualifying service funded by the program, if the participant would be no longer eligible to receive a qualifying service in the future.
- Otherwise, after they have not received a qualifying service funded by the program for 90 consecutive calendar days and is not scheduled to receive future services.

Exit days are applied retroactively to the last date the participant received a qualifying service.

<aside>
💡

**DOL/WIOA Reporting**

For the purposes of DOL/WIOA reporting, participants are not deemed to have exited until at least 90 days have elapsed since the participant last received services (not including self-service, information-only, or follow-up services) and there are no plans to provide the participant with future services.

DOL/WIOA participants are deemed to have a Common Exit when a participant, enrolled in multiple partner programs, has not received services from any DOL administered program in which the participant is enrolled, to which the common exit policy applies, for at least 90 days, and no future services are planned.

</aside>

## Post-Exit Followup

Outcomes for Performance Participants will be tracked:

- For 1 year post-graduation, for participants who were enrolled in secondary or postsecondary education while in the program.
- Otherwise, for 1 year after exiting the program.
