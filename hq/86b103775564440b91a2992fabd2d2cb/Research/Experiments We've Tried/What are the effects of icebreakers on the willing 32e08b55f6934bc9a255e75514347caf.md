# What are the effects of icebreakers on the willingness of students to ask for help from mentors, and the chance a student will meet new friends at the event? Effects of different types of icebreakers.

Result: At events which hosted icebreakers, students were 14% more likely to say they met a new friend at an event, and staff reported meeting 10% more of the event's participants.

We could not find a difference between the icebreakers we tried (group rock-paper-scissors, random group pitches, random group two-truths-and-a-lie, random group unique-and-shared).
