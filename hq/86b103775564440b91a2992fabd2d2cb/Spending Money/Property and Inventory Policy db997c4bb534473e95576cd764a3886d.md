# Property and Inventory Policy

## Prior to Purchase

You must request pre-approval from your manager (for employees) or CodeDay contact (for contractors, sub-recipients, or volunteers) prior to making an equipment purchase. Please include:

- What are you purchasing?
- What is the purpose of the property?
- Which staff will use the property?
- Where will the property be located?
- What percentage of the time will the property be used for each program or grant?
- If technology such as computers, laptops, tablets, etc. are to be purchased, please indicate the model. CodeDay will ensure it is compatible with security standards, and that the operating system and software are licensed for business use.

## Required Notification

Subrecipients and contractors must notify accounts@codeday.org on the following occasions:

- When new property arrives.
- When status changes to the property occur, including but not limited to:
    - Change of primary location.
    - Change in assigned staff.
    - Item no longer functions properly/is broken.
    - If any discrepancies in the records are noted.
- Missing, damaged, or stolen property.
- When property is no longer needed or is ready for disposal.

## Inventory

CodeDay will send you an inventory tag which must be affixed to all property. Property assigned to you must be properly tagged and easy to locate at all times.

CodeDay will maintain inventory records which include the information as prescribed in 2 CFR 200.313(d)(1):

- Description of property
- Use and condition
- Serial number
- Location
- Title holder, if any
- Acquisition date, cost, and records (such as receipts)
- Source of funding and percentage of Federal funds
- Disposition date and sale price
- Tag number

CodeDay’s sub-recipients and contractors must maintain their own inventory log; CodeDay Accounting will also maintain separate inventory records for CodeDay, its sub-recipients and contractors. CodeDay and its sub-recipients and contractors must conduct an inventory at least every two years.

## Items At Risk of Loss

All equipment except for furniture and fixtures are most at risk of loss. CodeDay Accounting will work with staff assigned to high-risk property to mitigate risks, including operational risks such as data security on portable computing devices.

## Property Disposal

Notify accounts@codeday.org when an item is ready for surplus or to be disposed; they will work with you to:

- Remove all sensitive, confidential, and personally identifying information
- Evaluate the condition of the property
- Dispose of the property to gain the highest possible return, which may include:
    - E-waste recycling
    - Offer the item to partners
    - Sell the item
- Record disposal status

CodeDay will retain purchase and disposal records for three years after disposal of property.

## Establishing Asset’s Useful Life and Calculating Depreciation

CodeDay will capitalize property valued at more than $5,000.

CodeDay calculates depreciation using the straight-line method. CodeDay is responsible for establishing and utilizing an appropriate useful life for assets acquired in less than new condition.
