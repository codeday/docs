# CONTRIBUTING.MD

## The Problem We’re Solving

Thousands of college students have only learned how to follow a predefined set of instructions called “classwork.” There’s no sense of discovery, no sense of “I did it myself.”

And the reality is that for most of their college education, they don’t even **get** a chance to get that “a-ha moment.” Many college curriculums don’t deal with contributing to real, large-scale projects, which means that these students are missing crucial problem-solving skills relating to software engineering.

## How We’re Solving This

CodeDay Labs provides “micro-internships” for college students. This means working on real issues on real-world projects. The best part about this is that we’re helping the open source community at the same time by providing opportunities for these students to work on ***open-source*** projects.

We have had a total of **8 sessions** that have personally impacted **200+ college students**, and a majority of them have said that they were ready to take on real-world coding problems after the program.

## How You Can Help

When you help contribute to CodeDay Labs, you’re supporting the open-source community by supporting this wave of students. It is also a way of instilling a sense of community among these students, making them feel like giving back to open-source.

Here’s how your projects comes in. By providing CodeDay Labs’s students with a few issues to work on in your project, you’re ensuring that they get real-world experience working on projects that leave an impact.

Here’s how it will work:

1. Create issues on your repository for CodeDay Labs students
    1. Read the guide and create your issues
        
        [Shaping An Issue](Shaping%20An%20Issue%202fa463fdfb1d48f9ba1d1d1ef2081d5f.md)
        
    2. Clearly mark in the description “For CodeDay Labs students only, do not assign to anyone else”
    3. **(optional)** Add a label to the issue called “CodeDay Labs”
2. Report back to your contact with links to the issues
3. Soon, you will receive a set of GitHub usernames from your contact. Assign the issue to these students.

## Benefits To Your Project

As we all know, it is difficult to get contributors to work on your projects

![chart.png](CONTRIBUTING%20MD/chart.png)

Even if you look at popular projects on GitHub, only the “top dogs” get the most number of contributors. ***It is difficult to get contributors for open source projects***.

CodeDay Init provides something that open source projects need most: ***new contributors***. Throughout our program, we’ve noticed that:

<aside>
🧑‍💻 ***59%*** of our students kept contributing to open source after the program

</aside>

<aside>
✨ ***75%*** of those students kept contributing to the **same** project they worked on

</aside>

By contributing to the program, you can get new, consistent contributors for your project. In one session alone we can have up to 16 students contributing to an open source project. That would be **128 new contributors** through the 8 sessions. We have sessions for every month of the school year, so the issues that you create will **NOT** be sitting for a long time. As we continue to scale CodeDay Init’s reach, the number of contributors you get for your project will only continue to rise.
