# If a Company is Interested

If a company is interested, the best next step is to schedule a call with them, which is almost always a better communication medium than email. Here's a suggested outline fro that call:

- Introduce yourself, and share your agenda for the call:
    - Summarize how CodeDay Labs works
    - Answer any questions they might have
    - Talk about ways we've worked with companies to promote volunteering as a mentor
- Basics of CodeDay Labs
    - It's a nonprofit summer program which matches students working on an open-source project, with mentors from industry
    - The time commitment is about 5 hours each week in July.
    - Any software engineers or project managers are usually a good fit. Past mentors have come from Google, Microsoft, Facebook, Apple, Netflix, Amazon, and more!
    - Students are mostly college juniors and seniors, and 70% of the students are underrepresented in software engineering.
    - Last year we had 1,500 applications for 300 spots, and we're expecting even more applications this year, so we're hoping for as many mentors as we can find.
- Do they have questions about CodeDay Labs? Answer their questions!
- Brainstorm how to get the word out about this volunteer opportunity. Some ways we've worked with companies in the past:
    - Given a 5-min presentation at the start of a company meeting or a tech team stand-up
    - Share information with a manager on a relevant team, who can pass it on to members of their team who are a good fit
    - Send over some materials to include in a company newsletter
- What do they think would work best?
